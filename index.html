About

Learn more about me

INDI Music Producer

I am a self taught indi music producer. I mainly use DAW software and a keyboard to create tracks however I have also played piano and drums for many years. Genres that I like:

  • Chill out
  • House/Electronic
  • Country
  • Hip hop
  • Film Score
  • Epic Music
  • Rock
  • Video Game Score

With an unwavering passion for experimenting with different genres, instruments, and production techniques, I am always on a journey to discover fresh, innovative ways to express emotion through music. Whether it's blending unexpected elements or reimagining traditional sounds, I find joy in pushing boundaries and breaking conventions.

For me, music is more than just a craft—it's a playground for creativity and self-expression. Their approach is all about embracing the unknown, taking risks, and allowing the music to evolve naturally. This adventurous spirit infuses every track with a unique energy, making my work a reflection of their curiosity and love for the art of sound. My past as a child was heavily influenced by the presense of audiobooks which is why you will also find audiobook music created by me here.

Software

Software that I use

DAW

Cubase

by Steinberg

As a DAW software i mainly use Cubase. However I also tried Ableton and FL Studio in the past.

Music Visualization

Blender

by Blender Foundation

Blender is a free and open source complete package for anything related to the 3D world. It can be used for complex 3D visualizations and it also provides its own python api allowing it to do pre-programmed visualizations.

After Effects

by Adobe

After Effects provides very strong support for visalizing music in a 2D space and also comes with some basic 3D features out of the box.

Raspberry Pi/ Arduino

Raspberry Pi Foundation/ Arduino AG

For a more physical approach i am also using Arduinos and Raspberry Pis to visualize music for example through LEDs. But there is really not limit here if you know how to provide enough electricity and computing power. I you are interested in this and which programming libraries provide the fastest access for operations on large amounts of LEDs you can visit my github.

Notation

MuseScore

by MuseScore Studio

From time to time I like to compose songs just for the piano. I have composed all of these using MuseScore. It is a simple but well structured program and is really easy to use.

Artificial Intelligence

Suno AI

by suno.com

I personally use Suno AI for artificial music creation however there might be some alternatives out there that are just as good.
